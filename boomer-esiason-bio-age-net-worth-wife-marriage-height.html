    Boomer Esiason Biography

    Boomer Esiason was born on 17th April 1961 in East Islip, New York. He is currently 62 years old and a citizen of the United States. Similarly, as of his birth date, he is an Aries.

    Moving on to his family, he was born to Norman Esiason and Irene Esiason. His father was of Norwegian and Swedish ancestry and a World War I veteran. Similarly, Irene, his mother, was a singer, dancer, and pianist.

    Additionally, Boomer has two sisters, Susan Esiason and Robin Esiason. As a child, he enjoyed football, basketball, and baseball. He also used to kick his pregnant mother’s womb, earning him the nickname Boomer before he was born.
    He attended East Islip High School and Timber Point Elementary School in East Islip, New York. He graduated from high school in 1979. In high school, he participated in football, baseball, and basketball. Following graduation, he enrolled at the University of Maryland.

    Similarly, he was coached at the collegiate level by Jerry Claiborne and Bobby Rose. While passing for 461 yards and 42 touchdowns, he set 17 school records. During his academic years, he received the Distinguished Alumnus Award.

    Career

    He was drafted by the Cincinnati Bengals following college and began his professional career in Maryland. He was selected in the second round with the 38th overall pick in the 1984 NFL Draft. He debuted in the NFL against the Houston Oilers.

    He was instrumental in his team’s 13-3 victory over Houston. He led the club to their second Super Bowl appearance three years later, but they were defeated by the San Francisco 49ers. He was named NFL MVP in 1988 and was selected to the Pro Bowl during his Bengals tenure.

    In 1993, he was traded to the New York Jets in exchange for a third-round pick. He sustained a serious concussion during the 1995 season. He will not be able to return to action until November 19. He signed with the Arizona Cardinals as a free agent in 1996. He considered retiring the following season, but chose to remain with the Bengals for one more season. Esiason quarterbacked the Cincinnati Bengals to four victories in five games. He had four games with at least 30 points.

    While still playing, Esiason worked as a color analyst for USA Network’s two-year broadcast. He has appeared in a variety of television shows and films, including Blue Bloods, Game Plan, Spin City, and Miss America. He also worked for ABC as a color commentator. From 2012 to 2015, Esiason presented the Super Bowl’s Greatest Commercials for the fourth consecutive year. Esiason won several trophies during his NFL career and is regarded as the 25th greatest quarterback of all time.
