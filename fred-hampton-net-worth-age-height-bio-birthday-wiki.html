    Top Facts about Fred Hampton

  • Fred Hampton was born in 1948 in Illinois.
  • He joined the Black Panther Party at age 19.
  • Hampton became a prominent leader and organizer within the party.
  • He advocated for racial equality and economic justice.
  • The FBI considered him a threat to national security.
  • In December 1969, he was killed by Chicago police during a raid.
  • His death sparked outrage and protests across the country.
  • Hampton’s legacy continues to inspire activism today.
  • A documentary about his life, “The Murder of Fred Hampton,” was released in 1971.
  • He is remembered as a martyr for the Black liberation movement.
